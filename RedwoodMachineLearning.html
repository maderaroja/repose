Predicting Fat Content of Canadian Cheese¶

by Nyanda Redwood

Introduction¶

Objective¶

The objective first entails doing an exploratory analysis and then predicting the level of fat in our target column, FatLevel, of each example.

Since the level of fat can either be "lower fat" or "higher fat" this question is a classification question. The positive label for this data is FatLevel.

Rationale¶

For cheese lovers who are also health conscious, knowing the level of fat in a type of cheese may assist in selection choices. This is especially the case in today's world of health-conscious eaters.

Description of the Dataset¶

The dataset deals with varous types of Canadian cheese. It was obtained from Kaggle and can be found here. It adheres to Open Government Licence (Canada).

Discussion¶

This analysis examined the cheese dataset related of different types of Canadian cheese. It aimed to create a machine learning model that predicted the fat content or level of each type of cheese based on each feature within the dataset. The fat level could only be predicted to one of two categories, lower and higher fat level. Consequently, this prediction problem was deemed to be a classification problem.

In order to carry out the analysis, it was necessary to wrangle features within the cheese dataset to compile relevant data in a form that readily and clearly facilitated the analysis, especially via vizualization.

Unexpectedly, there were errors in the wrangling of the dataset that led to column dropping. However, strong efforts were made to drop the least amount of columns. Still, of the remaining columns, missing categorical features were unsuccessfully imputed. This resulted in warnings of mismatched features, which could be of slight to severe consequences in our metric scores.

I, unsuccessfully, tried a lot of different ideas as Veined cheese piqued my curiosity, see below. However,I think for the unsuccessfull categorical transformations I could have tried a different method such as finding the value "ON" that seemed to have been the issue and drop it from these features. It possibly could have been found by using.unique().

Another question that could be explored is: "what features predict the production Veined cheese?" It seems that outside the world of cheese connoisseurs Veined cheese is rarely known. Thus, knowing about features that lead to the production of it could provide insight on this type of cheese.
